Activity 10 – Reheating

Bacteria can grow in food that is reheated too slowly. If you reheat previously cooked and cooled potentially hazardous food, you must reheat it to 75°C or hotter.

You should aim to reheat food to 75°C within a maximum of two hours to minimise the amount of time that food is at temperatures that favour the growth of bacteria or formation of toxins.

Pre-cooked food commercially supplied must be reheated in accordance with the procedures set out in the manufacturer’s instructions.

After reheating to 75°C the food can be cooled to a safe temperature prior to consumption.

Hazards

bacteria that may have survived the cooking process or bacteria that may have been introduced since the cooking process can multiply if potentially hazardous food is reheated too slowly;

Insufficient volume of water in the large pot or water bath may not cover the pouches and lead to uneven heat transfer.

Overcrowding of pouches in the large pot or water bath can interfere with the transfer of heat to the product and slow the process so that the food does not reach 75°C within two hour or less.

Food items that are solid or clumped together may not transfer heat to the core of the product efficiently. This could lead to uneven heating or cold spots.

bacteria can multiply in potentially hazardous food that is not reheated to 75°C or above;

bacteria from unclean equipment or utensils may contaminate reheated food;

foreign objects can contaminate uncovered or unprotected food;

 

Controls and monitoring

 

always use clean & sanitised equipment and utensils to handle reheated food;

always use a clean & sanitised thermometer to check the temperature of the food.

always use a clean utensil for taste testing and never reuse;

potentially hazardous food that has been cooked and cooled should only be reheated once.

food may be reheated in a microwave, but operators should be aware of the possibility of uneven heating using this technique.

 

Simmer in the Bag:

When reheating food in a large pot or water bath, follow the instructions supplied by the food manufacturer.

Fill the large pot or water bath with sufficient hot water, allowing enough space for the bags to be submerged without the water boiling over the side.

Bring the water to the boil. Carefully place bags into the water (using tongs). Adjust heat to maintain a gentle simmer.

Ensure the bags remain free moving in the water while reheating. Direct contact with the base of the heating vessel could result in split bags.

Heat contents until core temperature reaches 75°C.

Stir potentially hazardous food or agitate pouches in the water bath during reheating to ensure the heat is evenly dispersed throughout the food;

Do not place more than four (4) large pouches or 8 small pouches (or equivalent) in the water bath or each large pot (18Lt) at one time. Allow plenty of space between pouches when reheating.

Using a thermometer (clean & sanitised), check that the temperature at the centre of potentially hazardous food being reheated reaches at least 75°C within two hours;

When checking the temperature of pouches in the water bath use the following procedure (recommended by the manufacturer):

Wearing protective gloves, and using a perforated spoon or scoop, remove the pouch from the water bath

Carefully squeeze the pouch to ensure there are no lumps or cold spots, especially with pouches of rice, pasta or vegetables.

Place the pouch on a clean cloth or paper towel. Fold the pouch over the probe of the digital thermometer and hold for one minute or until the temperature reading stabilises.

If the temperature is more than 75°C record the temperature on the heating record and serve or hot hold the food.

If the temperature is less than 75°C return the pouch to the water bath and continue to heat.

 

Microwave Oven:

Heat, unopened, in the microwave or

Decant into an approprate clean & sanitised container and microwave

Heat until the core temperature of the food is 75°C.

Puree for babies must be reheated quickly to at least 75°C in the microwave oven. It must then be cooled to a safe temperature for consumption and served immediately

maintain reheated potentially hazardous food at 75°C or above until required for service;

Stove Top/ Electric frypan Reheating

Open bag and transfer contents to an appropriate non-stick pan.

Stir until core temperature of the contents reaches 75°C.

Oven Reheating

● Decant into an appropriate container, if required

● Reheat until the core is 75°C

● Cover with foil if necessary

 

 

Corrective actions

if, upon opening the pouch, the core temperature of the food is found to be below 75°C transfer the food to a suitably sized, microwave oven proof container and continue to heat, in the microwave oven , on stove to the food is above 75°C.

If when reheating on the stove top, in an electric fry pan or in the oven if the core temperature of the food is below 75°C continue reheating until 75°C or above is achieved.

discard potentially hazardous food if the temperature at the centre has not reached 75°C in two hours;

use immediately any potentially hazardous food that has been held between 5°C and 60°C for up to 2 hours, but discard food that has been held between 5°C and 60°C for more than two (2) hours;

discard reheated food if left over;
