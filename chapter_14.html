Activity 13 – Transporting food

This activity covers temperature-controlled transporting of Hot and Cold food items from our commercial kitchen to our sister centre (Brewer St).

It is essential that our vehicles and equipment used to transport food are designed and constructed to protect the food.

Hazards

dust, dirt, chemicals, pests or other foreign objects may contaminate unprotected food;

food poisoning bacteria can multiply if potentially hazardous food is transported between 5°C and 60°C; and

food poisoning bacteria can be transferred from raw food to ready-to-eat food if transported incorrectly. This is called cross contamination.

Controls and Monitoring

All foods for transport are kept in sealed plastic containers or securely wrapped to protect food from becoming contaminated.

all ready-to-eat food must be kept separate from raw food.

All foods for transport will be packed into appropriately insulated boxes and transported either hot (>60°C) or cold (<5°C).

check that cold food is transported at 5°C or colder.

if food is transported between 5°C and 60°C discard after two hours.

make sure that the food is transported away from any chemicals that might contaminate the food; and

make sure that the delivery vehicle and transport boxes are clean, and food is packaged correctly.

 

Corrective Actions

throw away food that has become contaminated (e.g. foreign objects, chemicals);

throw away ready-to-eat food that has been contaminated by raw food;

potentially hazardous food that has been kept between 5°C and 60°C for less than two hours (cumulative time) must be refrigerated or used immediately;

potentially hazardous food that has been kept between 5°C and 60°C for longer than two hours must be discarded;

review the food transport and other relevant activities if potentially hazardous food is being held between 5°C and 60°C for two (2) hours or longer, including any such time prior to and after transport and before using the food.
